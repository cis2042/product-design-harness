# Code Of Conduct

## Standard

The UX3 community welcomes disagreement about product judgment. Critique the
claim, evidence, rule, or trade-off; do not attack the person presenting it.

Expected behavior:

- make room for different disciplines, cultures, languages, and experience;
- state uncertainty and conflicts of interest;
- protect customer, participant, and contributor privacy;
- give specific, actionable review feedback;
- preserve counter-evidence instead of suppressing inconvenient results; and
- accept that maintainers may reject a polished contribution that lacks
  decision impact or evidence.

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, discrimination, threats, sexualized
conduct, deliberate exposure of private information, evidence fabrication,
retaliation, or sustained disruption of project work.

## Scope

This standard applies in repository Issues, Pull Requests, Discussions,
community events, and project-related private communication.

## Reporting And Enforcement

For immediate platform abuse, use GitHub reporting and blocking controls. For
a project-specific sensitive report, contact the repository owner through the
private contact method on the owner's GitHub profile. Do not publish private
evidence or identifying information in an Issue.

Maintainers may edit or remove content, lock conversations, reject a
contribution, temporarily limit participation, or ban a participant. Responses
will be proportional to impact, repetition, and risk. Maintainers involved in
the report must recuse themselves when another maintainer is available.
